# Research & Discovery: Board Meeting Platform Redesign

## Problem Statement
Executive board members struggle with digital platforms that prioritize document storage over actionable intelligence. Current systems create cognitive load through fragmented navigation, poor action item visibility, and non-intuitive workflows, leading to delayed strategic execution.

## Competitive Analysis Summary
| Tool | Strengths | Weaknesses |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Diligent** | Secure, established, reliable. | Dated UI, high complexity, stiff navigation. |
| **OnBoard** | Modern interface, good mobile app. | Action items feel like an afterthought, voting is separate from agenda. |
| **BoardEffect** | Good for non-profits, simple. | Lacks advanced tracking and real-time meeting tools. |

## User Personas

### 1. Board Member: Arthur (The Strategist)
- **Role**: Chairperson / Non-Exec Director.
- **Goals**: Quick review of board packs, clear sight of resolutions, efficient meeting leading.
- **Frustrations**: Receiving 500-page PDFs, not knowing what actions are pending from last quarter.

### 2. Company Secretary: Sarah (The Organizer)
- **Role**: Governance Officer.
- **Goals**: Seamless pack distribution, accurate minute-taking, tracking resolution outcomes.
- **Frustrations**: Chasing directors for votes, manually tracking action items across spreadsheets.

## Key Friction Points
1. **The "Board Pack" Wall**: Navigating hundreds of pages without clear linking to the agenda.
2. **Post-Meeting "Black Hole"**: Actions are buried in PDF minutes; no live dashboard for progress.
3. **Voting Friction**: Voting requires navigating away from the document being discussed.
